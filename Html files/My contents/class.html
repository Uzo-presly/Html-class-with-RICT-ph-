The Five Best Cable Cars

  1. Peak2peak Gondola (Whistler Canada)

    2. This Gondola is 4.4 kilometers and connects two mountain's peaks.

    .

  2. Titlis Rotair (Mount Titlis, Switzerland)

    3. On this gondola passengers are whisked to the top of Mount Titlis some 3,020 meters (9,908 feet) above sea level.

    .

  3. Mi Teleferico (La Paz. Molivia)

    4. This urban gondola is designed to ferry passengers from the center of La Paz to the district of El Alto, high above the cities. Prior to its installation, such a journey was accomplished via a narrow zig-zagging route.

    .

  4. Sugarloaf Mountain Gondola (Rio De Janeiro, Brazil)

    5. It is on record that over 37 million persons have ridden upon this gondola since its first operation in 1912. With each cabin able to carry 65 passengers, the gondola's journey is split in three phases; each of three minutes respectively.

    .

  5. Vanoise Express (Les Arcs, France)

    6. This gondola connects the French ski resorts of Les Arcs and La Plagne. In 2013, when her 10th anniversary was celebrated, French tight-rope walkers Julien Millot and Tancrede Melet, crossed a 186-foot span between two gondola cars.
